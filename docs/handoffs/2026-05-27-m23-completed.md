## Research findings (escalate to M2.5)

### Finding 1: Ranking divergence vs paper

| | #1 | #2 | #3 | #4 |
|--|----|----|----|----|
| **Paper** | CatBoost 0.9857 | LightGBM 0.9849 | XGBoost 0.9840 | HistGBT 0.9839 |
| **Ours** | LightGBM 0.9818 | HistGBT 0.9817 | CatBoost 0.9788 | XGBoost 0.9749 |

XGBoost gap is the largest (0.91%) and HistGBT gap is the smallest (0.22%),
the inverse of what paper shows. Most likely explanation: `impute_nulls` missing
(unknowns #10 1st candidate) affects XGBoost/CatBoost more than LightGBM/HistGBT
due to different internal NaN split strategies. Documented as unknowns #10 7th
candidate.

### Finding 2: Cross-model importance reveals model-specific signals

Top 5 feature importance per model:

| Rank | LightGBM | XGBoost | CatBoost |
|------|----------|---------|----------|
| 1 | **building_id** | meter_reading | meter_reading |
| 2 | lag_ratio_1 | lag_ratio_168 | lag_ratio_1 |
| 3 | meter_reading | **Residual_savgol** | **Residual_savgol** |
| 4 | lag_ratio_-1 | lag_ratio_1 | lag_ratio_-1 |
| 5 | dayofyear | lag_ratio_19 | building_id |

HistGBT: sklearn 1.7 does not expose `feature_importances_`; excluded.

**Common top 10 across 3 models**: 6/10 (`meter_reading`, `building_id`,
`lag_ratio_{1,-1,168,-168}`, `Residual_savgol_w5p3`)

**Design implications**:
- LightGBM strength: building-level personalization (`building_id` rank 1)
- XGBoost/CatBoost strength: absolute meter level + SavGol smoothing
- Ensemble lift = 6 common features (baseline signal) + 4 model-specific
  features (marginal lift per model)
- Paper §2.3.4 "Each model has its own strengths and weaknesses" — this is the
  first quantitative evidence. Paper does not show cross-model importance.

Documented in unknowns #13.

### Finding 3: Paper §2.2.4 "SavGol no effect" may only apply to LightGBM

- LightGBM: `Residual_savgol_w5p3` rank 6 (split count = 105)
- XGBoost: **rank 3** (top 5)
- CatBoost: **rank 3** (top 5)

Paper §2.2.4 says "did not show apparent positive effect" — this was likely
based on LightGBM gain-based or permutation importance only. M2.5 ablation must
quantify per-model ΔAUC, not just LightGBM.

Documented in unknowns #12 (cross-model observation added).

## Methodology lessons (cumulative — 6 total)

| # | Milestone | Lesson |
|---|-----------|--------|
| 1 | M2.2.0 | Tree-based monotonic invariance: AUC invariant to monotonic feature transforms |
| 2 | M2.2.a | sklearn `n_init` silent default change (1.4+: `'auto'` with k-means++ = 1, not 10) |
| 3 | M2.2.c | SavGol leading-NaN: chain `.ffill().bfill().fillna(0)`, not just `.ffill()` |
| 4 | M2.2.d | Pearson corr is a poor proxy for tree feature importance (non-linear capture) |
| 5 | M2.2.e | Centered SavGol is legitimate for batch tasks; future-info ≠ leakage in historical inference |
| 6 | **M2.3** | **Cross-model importance divergence reveals ensemble theoretical basis**: same features, different models → different top-rank features; 6/10 common = baseline, 4/10 model-specific = marginal lift |

## Next session plan: M2.4 (post-processing + refit)

Per `docs/m2-plan.md` M2.4 section.

### Two post-processing rules (from Modeling notebook Cell 14)

**Rule 1** — `meter_reading == 1` is definitively anomalous:
```python
predictions[val['meter_reading'] == 1] = 1
```

**Rule 2** — time series start/end points are definitively normal:
```python
# Start: day 1, specific building ID range (not all buildings)
predictions[(val['dayofyear'] == 1) & ((val['building_id'] > 145) | (val['building_id'] < 105))] = 0
# End: last hour(s) of the year
predictions[val['dayofyear'] > 366.9583] = 0
```

Rule 2's building-ID condition (`> 145 | < 105`) means buildings 105–145 are
**excluded** from the day-1 override. This is not generic "first row per building"
but a hardcoded filter for LEAD's specific 200-building subset.

### M2.4 task outline

1. Apply Rule 1 + Rule 2 to ensemble predictions on val set; print ΔAUC
2. Final refit: `X_all` = downsampled train + val combined; refit all 4 models
3. Generate test set predictions → `data/processed/test_predictions.csv`
4. Evaluate post-processing AUC vs pre-processing (direction check)
